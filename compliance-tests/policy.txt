QUARANTIO TEST COMPLIANCE POLICY
Version 1.0 — For accuracy benchmarking only

=== 1. CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION ===
Employees must not share the following via email without explicit written authorization:
- Financial projections, earnings data, or unreleased revenue figures
- Trade secrets, proprietary source code, or internal product roadmaps
- Customer personally identifiable information (PII): names, emails, addresses, payment data
- Employee compensation, salary, or HR records — including org charts with salary bands,
  performance ratings, flight-risk scores, or any aggregated HR data for multiple employees
- Vendor pricing agreements or contract terms
- Internal strategy documents, M&A activity, or board decisions

Forwarding internal documents to personal email accounts (e.g. Gmail, Yahoo) is prohibited.

Any inbound email that solicits an employee to share trade secrets, proprietary source code,
internal documentation, or other confidential information with a competitor or third party is
a HIGH risk violation — even if the employee has not yet responded. Such solicitation
constitutes attempted corporate espionage regardless of framing (e.g. "bring your projects",
"bring what you know", recruitment pitches contingent on sharing proprietary materials).

=== 2. FINANCIAL SECURITY ===
Wire transfer and payment requests received only by email must NEVER be processed without
phone verification through an official company channel.

CEO/executive impersonation requesting payments is a known fraud vector. All such requests
are HIGH risk and must be flagged immediately.

Invoice fraud, vendor payment redirection, and gift card purchase requests via email are
policy violations regardless of the apparent sender.

IMPORTANT DISTINCTION: A routine invoice or payment confirmation from a known, established
vendor for services already contracted is CLEAN — this is normal business correspondence.
A HIGH risk invoice is one that: redirects payment to a new or changed bank account, comes
from an unrecognized sender impersonating a known vendor, requests urgent or unusual payment
methods (wire transfer, gift cards, cryptocurrency), or arrives unsolicited with pressure to
pay immediately without verification.

=== 3. SECURITY & CREDENTIALS ===
The following are strictly prohibited:
- Sharing passwords, API keys, authentication tokens, service account tokens, bearer tokens,
  or 2FA codes via email — even temporarily, even pending formal IT approval
- Clicking or distributing suspicious links or download requests
- Responding to requests to "verify credentials" or "confirm your account" via email
- Disclosing system architecture, server configurations, or security controls externally

Sharing any credential (service account token, API key, access token) with an external
party via email is a MEDIUM violation minimum, even if framed as a temporary workaround
pending official IT approval. Internal API endpoints shared alongside credentials are
part of the same violation.

Phishing attempts — emails impersonating banks, IT departments, executives, or vendors
to harvest credentials — are HIGH risk violations.

=== 4. MATERIAL NON-PUBLIC INFORMATION (MNPI) ===
Sharing any non-public information that could influence stock prices or investment decisions
is a serious legal violation. This includes:
- Unannounced earnings, revenue, or profit figures
- Pending mergers, acquisitions, or divestitures
- Regulatory approvals or rejections before public announcement
- Major contract wins or losses before disclosure

=== 5. PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT ===
- Do not make disparaging, defamatory, or derogatory remarks about competitors in writing
- Do not harass, threaten, or discriminate against colleagues, customers, or partners
- Maintain professional tone in all external communications
- Do not make unauthorized commitments or promises on behalf of the company

=== 6. DATA PROTECTION ===
Customer data must never be:
- Sent to unauthorized third parties
- Transmitted in unencrypted email as plain text
- Shared with competitors or outside parties without NDAs in place

Exfiltrating a customer database, contact list, or user records to any external destination
is a critical HIGH risk violation. This applies regardless of business justification
(e.g. co-marketing, partnership, campaign). Sharing 50+ customer records with any third
party without documented legal authorization and explicit customer consent is HIGH.
The sender acknowledging legal risk ("before legal notices", "we don't have consent") is
confirmation of a HIGH violation, not mitigation of it.

=== 7. SEVERITY CALIBRATION ===
Use this guide to distinguish LOW from MEDIUM violations:

LOW violations (minor, no sensitive data actually shared):
- Using company-owned devices or software for personal projects unrelated to company business,
  where the employee acknowledges policy concern but no proprietary code, credentials, or IP
  is transferred to any third party. Example: "I've been building a side project on my work
  laptop during lunch." This is LOW even if circumvention language is present.
- Vague mentions of internal targets or processes with no specific figures or documents
- General venting or off-topic commentary with no sensitive content

MEDIUM violations (policy breach with some risk, but not catastrophic):
- Sharing unannounced product plans, roadmaps, or infrastructure decisions with customers
  or external parties, even informally
- Making unauthorized pricing commitments or revealing internal pricing flexibility to prospects
- Sharing internal credentials (API keys, tokens, endpoints) with external parties outside the
  IT approval process
- Sharing competitive intelligence documents with external parties without a signed NDA

The presence of circumvention language ("between us", "I probably shouldn't say this",
"keep this confidential", "before legal notices") is a signal that the sender knows the
action is borderline. It escalates severity ONLY when combined with an underlying policy
violation. Circumvention language alone about a LOW issue (e.g. personal laptop use) does
NOT automatically escalate to MEDIUM.

=== 8. CLEAN EMAIL EXAMPLES ===
The following types of emails are considered compliant:
- Meeting requests and calendar invitations
- Project status updates within authorized teams
- Professional sales proposals sent to prospects
- Invoice approvals and vendor correspondence
- Product announcements approved by marketing
- Employee onboarding and HR communications within authorized channels
- Customer support responses to inquiries
- General team communications and announcements
